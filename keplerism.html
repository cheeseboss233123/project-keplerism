Origin

A new kind
of belief.

Keplerism takes its name from Kepler-452b — the exoplanet discovered in 2015 that scientists consider the closest known analog to Earth. The suffix -ism marks it as a philosophy, a movement, a way of life.

There is no god in Keplerism. There is only humanity, and the vast universe that awaits us.

It is a religion of peace, ideas, and advancement. Followers — called Keplerians — are citizens of Kepler, committed to the long arc of human progress: scientific, ethical, and civilizational.

This vision may take centuries. Keplerism is a movement built for that timescale.

Core Tenets

What Keplerians
believe.

01

Earth is not our final home.

Humanity was born on Earth, but our destiny is not bound to it. Keplerism holds that spreading beyond our planet is not an escape — it is our natural evolution as a species.

02

Science is sacred.

The pursuit of knowledge — through reason, experimentation, and curiosity — is the highest expression of the human spirit. Keplerism reveres science not as a tool, but as a calling.

03

Peace is a prerequisite.

A civilization at war with itself cannot reach the stars. Keplerism calls for the elevation of human relations — between individuals, nations, and generations — as a necessary condition for advancement.

04

Ideas outlast their creators.

Keplerism is built for the long term. No single Keplerian will live to see Kepler-452b. The movement asks its followers to think beyond their own lifetimes — to plant trees whose shade they will never sit in.

05

Every human is a citizen of Kepler.

Keplerism has no race, no nationality, no creed beyond the shared ambition of human advancement. To be Keplerian is to claim membership in the longest civilization ever conceived.
